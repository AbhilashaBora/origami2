Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels are known as the ship of desert.

Chameleon

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads
  • Chameleons come in a lot of different shapes and sizes
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons have been present with humans since ages.
  • Pigeons are known as "Rats of the Sky".
  • Pigeons have fought alongside humans in World War I and II.

Teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Panda

  • They have a great camouflage for their environment.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.

Cicada

  • Cicadas live on all continents except Antarctica.
  • Some cicadas could explode with fungus.
  • Cicadas can't eat, they get nutrients from tree roots.
